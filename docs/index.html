A ledger of what funds actually costPrepared 2026-07-06

The expense ratio is the front door. Behind it sit brokerage commissions, soft dollar arrangements, bid-ask spreads, and market impact — costs that reduce a fund's return before its NAV is ever struck. The data to estimate them has been sitting in SEC filings since 2018. This ledger aggregates it, fund by fund, with every figure tagged to its source filing or estimation model.

Figures are in basis points per year. "Reported hidden" counts only costs the fund itself filed in dollars (brokerage commissions and disclosed soft-dollar commissions from N-CEN); it is the number no model can be blamed for. "Estimated total" adds modeled spread and market-impact ranges on top, tagged as estimates because that is what they are. The methodology and its limitations are published in full.
